This plot was made using scatter plot and explicitly having a regression line From this plot we can see how our revenue is tending to increase along with the popularity at the same time we can also see that there are few outliers.

Although it is observed that the revenue tends to increase, still, there are few movies having high popularity than that of others couldn't make a lot of money!

There were few movies which had outperformed other movies in terms of revenue and these movies were having a very little popularity!

Here I plotted a new graph again, with regplot which automatically takes care of regression line.¶

Here is was also observed that the results of the above plot viz (Plot 3) are repeated, also we could see that the regression line generated was also proving the fact that as the popularity increases, the revenue also increases.

diff_bug_rev = movies_df['revenue'] - movies_df['budget']
profit = sum(diff_bug_rev > 0)

Limitations¶

There were few limitations to the analysis based on the given data.

  1. First Since we had already dropped out many columns out of our analysis since they were irrelevant for our analysis.
  2. Many of the columns were simply either strings or urls or some basic info which we could have got from genres. Also many columns had a large number of empty entries which is not a good sign for conducting proper data analysis over it.
  3. Inconsistency in the column of Genres, as there were multiple genres for a single movie, also in some cases there were no entries which we had cleared out in the beginning.
  4. In the column of popularity the range was too large to carry our analysis on. Ex. Max value for range came out to be 32 and min was 0. Also, max value was really very far awar from the mean value of 1.
  5. Popularity was not defined on a proper scale and it was dependent on many factors of how many people voted for it on one day, etc.
  6. The two columns of revenue_adj and budget_adj were not so clear about what the TMDb wanted to convey from it and there was not a lot of description given for it either over their website.

Other Variables¶

Conclusions¶

Question 1: How does movies vary according to the genre? Here we could visualize the data of the movies, how many movies belonged to each of the genres listed in their columns. Here it was an observational question so there is neither causation nor correlation. Its just for our information to know which genre has most number of movies.

Question 2: How does revenue vary with popularity? Here we could see how popularity affected the revenue of the movie (Plot 2, 3 and 4). It was visible from our (Plot 3) it's not really true that all the movies which are popular made good money, and also, its not true that movies which were not popular were bad at revenue. It was visible how the movies which were not really very popular also generated good amount of revenue. However, we could see that as the popularity increased the revenue tends increase as well. So, we can conclude that Correlation doesn't imply causation

If there are few movies which are having high popularity needn't imply they would fetch good amount of money and vice versa.¶

At the end I had plotted a bar graph (Plot 5) and a pie chart (Plot 6) to depict how many of all these movies were actually profitable and we could see that only 34% of all the movies that were there could make profits! This point could be very important for someone who really needs to understand how movies really work out financially!
